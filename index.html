Dr. Albert Einstein
  • 1879 - Albert Einstein is born in Ulm, Germany.
  • 1896 - Einstein graduates from the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology in Zurich, Switzerland, with a degree in physics and mathematics.
  • 1900 - Einstein graduates from the University of Zurich with a teaching diploma in mathematics and physics. He begins work as a tutor and teacher in Switzerland.
  • 1902 - Einstein starts working as a technical assistant at the Swiss Patent Office in Bern.
  • 1905 - Einstein publishes five groundbreaking papers, including the theory of special relativity, the photoelectric effect, and E=mcÂ². These papers establish him as one of the most important physicists of the time.
  • 1915 - Einstein publishes his theory of general relativity, which becomes one of the pillars of modern physics.
  • 1919 - Einstein becomes world-famous when his theory of general relativity is confirmed by an experiment during a total solar eclipse.
  • 1921 - Einstein receives the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1925 - Einstein publishes a paper with his colleagues Boris Podolsky and Nathan Rosen, outlining what would become known as the EPR paradox, which challenged the principles of quantum mechanics.
  • 1929 - Einstein makes his first trip to the United States as a permanent resident, accepting a position at the Institute for Advanced Study at Princeton University in New Jersey.
  • 1933 - Adolf Hitler becomes Chancellor of Germany, and Einstein renounces his German citizenship and moves to the United States to avoid persecution due to his Jewish heritage.
  • 1939 - Einstein signs a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt warning of the possibility of Nazi Germany developing an atomic bomb, and urging the U.S. to begin its own atomic research program.
  • 1945 - The atomic bombs are dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, leading Einstein to become a vocal advocate for nuclear disarmament.
  • 1948 - Einstein receives the title of "Righteous Among the Nations" from Yad Vashem, the Holocaust Martyrs' and Heroes' Remembrance Authority in Israel, for his outspokenness against anti-Semitism and Nazism.
  • 1952 - Einstein is offered the presidency of Israel but declines.
  • 1955 - Einstein passes away on April 18 at the age of 76 in Princeton, New Jersey.
